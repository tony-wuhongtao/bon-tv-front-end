  • The Railway that Links China & Rotterdam

    Since 2013, the Chinese government has been investing tens of billions into the improvement of Eurasian rail connections and facilities, and Rotterdam in turn is actively involved in rail. The city would be a logical starting and end point for a Eurasian route – particularly considering its position as a hub port. By now, a growing number of companies have become interested in rail connections. These include the Rotterdam-based transport firm G&D Europe, established in 2010 by Jan-Paul Vegt and his Chinese business partner Yonggao Liu. These two young entrepreneurs met each other during their study at business school, and they set up a company together. This summer (2016), the company will be sending its first container by train to China. Eurasian rail transport forms an attractive transport option for Vegt and Liu. For startup companies, every decision made is crucial. G&D's story will give their entrepreneur peers an inspiration of cross-border and cross-cultural cooperation.
